fps games

Video games was a big part of my life for quite long time until I quit playing theme (I may get into the details about this decision). One of those video game genres I have played for a long time is called first-person shooter (FPS) games which is a very popular game type worldwide. The details of it can be searched throughly, but it is basically a game type where the player is the first-person from the perspective of where you can see the game world and yes, the player is shooting at targets basically, hence the name. I think its closest to the real life by means of seeing the world through the characters' eyes in these games.

My journey with FPS games started in 1999 I guess. As far as I started playing the infamous Half-Life game in an internet-cafe where we would go with my older brother to play this game multi-player. Then it changed into Counter-Strike and others. As a child it was really entertaining and addictive. Also, I may say that I was generally a good player in most of the games and FPS genre is one of them. However, sometimes I take stuff seriously as it was the case for FPS games. By saying "taking stuff seriously", I mean that I don't play for fun solely, I compete and I compete seriously. Its not even in multi-player games only, I get into the character in single player games also and take those also serious as well. I think I am just trying to get the most out of those games by acting so. I don't want to spend my time just for fun, it feel just wrong, unncessary, easy, shallow; I don't know you multiply these words and try to understand the picture. I would even watch my gameplay replays in some other game (not FPS though). I remember saying to one of my frinds at that time who was also playing computer games with me, that I get fun when I win., something along these lines, he was suggesting to take it easy and have fun (which is maybe more healthier way to think about computer games) but I wanted to win.
